With the discovery of astrophysical neutrinos and gravitational waves, we have entered into a new era of mutli-messenger astrophysics. Flares, shocks, and other non-thermal processes can accelerate subatomic particles to the highest energies ever observed. Neutrinos are produced as the byproducts of hadronic acceleration. We can also use these high-energy neutrinos to explore fundamental physics interactions at energies inaccessible to accelerators here on Earth. We use radio detection techniques to search for these rare particles. Neutrinos and cosmic rays generate fast radio sparks when they initiate showers in ice or air.
